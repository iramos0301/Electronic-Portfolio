Judo (2022 - 2023)

Judo is a sport where people attempt to throw or pin each other get points to win. High school judo has team competitions and individual competitions. Judo was the first sport and club that I joined out of a whim during high school. It was a memorable experience given that I had to balance going to practice everyday along with my academics and early college. Despite this, I still persevered and was able to practice judo for 2 seasons during my freshman and sophomore year. During judo, we had to endure physically taxing workouts, spars with other judokas, and the responsibility of going to every practice and competition. Through these experiences, I was able to learn how to cooperate and grow with my team members in a more competitive sense while being able to challenge myself to take on more responsibilities besides school. Overall, judo was a great experience that taught me many life lessons like being consistent, building long lasting friendships through rivalries, and having discipline.

Phi Theta Kappa (2021 - 2025)

Phi Theta Kappa is a honor society that's at the collegiate level. PTK members are recognized for academic achievements and strive for scholarship, laedership, felllowship, and service. PTK offers these traits through various activities and events such as community service, PTK catalyst (an event that brings together members for professional development), scholarships offered by PTK, and encourages members to uphold their membership by participating in their community that presents these traits. Through PTK, I started becoming more involved with my community. This is mainly because of the community service opportunities that were given to the members and required community service hours. In addition to becoming more involved in my community, I was able to grow as a person. During some PTK events, there'd be professional speakers that helped to inspire and bring a new perspective on life that helped to encourage me to grow further.
